This project unfolds as a game. When the daily narratives and experiences we rely on become fragmented and lose coherence, and when our self-perception is disrupted and reshaped by external data…

This project introduces the concept of "Diagnosis." Here, "Diagnosis" does not mean labeling specific diseases or fixed identities; instead, it explores how external data reshapes one's internal experience, describing measurable physical states such as humidity, heart rate, and atmospheric pressure that shape one's perceptual experiences. Data continuously reads and guides, causing the individual to drift imperceptibly and experience a distorted breakdown.

Drawing on diary entries and physical data, the project constructs a virtual character whose perceptual logic and lived experience are continually reorganized through ongoing data interventions. Players piece together fragmented information within the game, engaging in a cognitive process of reconstruction and inference regarding the character’s true state of existence.

This project also engages with the fluidity of narrative media: Beyond the game mechanics, I experiment with spoken storytelling and video to fragment the original story into multiple paths. This non-linear narrative structure not only challenges the traditional text-centered logic, but also opens up future possibilities for exploring alternative media to convey the ruptures, delays, and reconstructions inherent in perceptual experience.

"Have you ever noticed that
perception is not evenly distributed?
It arranges itself in response to events,
a random composition of senses crossing over each other,
shifting in proportion.
They interlock like intricate gears,
continuously reconfiguring,
penetrating,

invading our awareness,
dismantling and reshaping reality,
ultimately determining how we experience this moment.
Perhaps,
perception itself is nothing more than a transient system, ceaselessly reshaped.”

Her anxiety dissolved the membrane between her and reality, leaving her exposed to a sharp, sticky discomfort.

As if draped in a damp cloth—fermenting, humid, and sour.

Things once silent seem to whisper: roots unfurling beneath the earth, an empire expanding in absolute stillness.

I dreamt of my body floating in a bathtub, rising and falling with the waves. My limbs disintegrated in the water, my dissolving skin transforming into an unfamiliar landscape.

I heard the sound of your skin moving.

She begins to rely on stillness, attempting to reset her perception.

These mycelial currents mimic the extracellular conductivity of fungi, their filaments entwining with the myelin sheaths of her neurons, allowing signals to pass seamlessly between the synthetic and the organic.

They do more than carry current—they whisper deep within her tissues, testing, reconfiguring the protocols through which she senses herself.

"Do you remember the language of your dreams? Do you remember the sound of your voice—are you sure it was your own?”

These intersecting fields breach her awareness, and her dreams begin to fragment, distort.

She dreams of ancient forests, of rocks weathered by time, of microbes slowly unfolding over eons.
