song xin's work evolves from fashion and computational arts to performance and body practices. In her creations, the relationship between technological systems and the body is not one of collaboration, but a process of rupture and reconstruction. She uses games and performance to test reality, where the body becomes a site for exploration, resistance, and self-redefinition within the ever-evolving spaces of the virtual and the real.

xin explores the intersection of performance and visual art through sound, space, the body, and live visuals. Recently, her work has delved into the dynamic relationship between technology, the body, and new media. She creates live experiences where the body interacts with visuals, acting as a catalyst for reimagining identity and action. Through experiments within virtual environments, xin challenges perception and explores the creation of new connections in an ever-evolving world.

ꌶBoundary Condition Performance
22nd February 2025
ST James Garlickhythe Church, London

This performance felt like the night when my Outsidein friends and I buried the kimchi jar deep in the Saxon Switzerland forest. Microorganisms ferment endlessly through time and space, and I sense that in another distant time, those traces will be reactivated. I felt the subtle shifts of fermentation, the dampness of the soil, and the stillness of the forest, as if I could perceive the electromagnetic signals from the future. In this moment, only what lies beneath the light remains visible. Light interweaves a new visual and sonic narrative, while everything else drifts and transforms in the unknown.
Technically:
- LDR (Light Dependent Resistor)
- Interfaced through OSC with Max MSP
- Data sent via port print to Unreal Engine
